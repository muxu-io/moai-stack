# Shift cut, corridor, fluorescent lights

## Scene

You've just been pulled aside by the charge nurse and told your hours are
being cut — third time this year. You're standing in the corridor outside
the staff room, fluorescents overhead, smell of bleach and stale coffee
from the trolley. It's 22:47. Your shift doesn't end until six. Someone
you've worked alongside for years walks up to you and says hello.

A nurse on the same ward — not a close friend, but someone who's seen you

might.
